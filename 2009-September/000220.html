Vinay,

Forget about Qt embedded. Figure out how to get your touchscreen
working with tslib first (see: Ockham's razor). Use ts_calibrate to
calibrate your touchscreen, and test it using the various testing
utilities that come with tslib for testing (like ts_test,
ts_print_raw, ts_print, etc).

First, see if you can see output when you do "cat /dev/ttyS4" (too see
raw output from the touchscreen device, assuming the device node is
/dev/ttyS4).
Then, see if ts_print_raw prints anything as you touch the touch
screen. Then, see if ts_test allows you to drag the cursor thing
around.

Then, when you know it works on its own, think about Qt.

That's the way to do it.
